You are viewing a laptop buying guide that will definitely help you to make a wise and confident decision when you go to buy a laptop.

Why am not I considering desktops on this page? Because they do not offer portability, which is a very important feature in today's era. This Laptop Buying Guide is focused towards the majority that need a decently big screen, good computing power and portability. We'll talk about desktops some other day.

With the above lines being said, let's have a look at some of the factors that will help you to find a laptop that best suits your needs (and your budget). These factors are: -


  1. OPERATING SYSTEM (OS)



    2. Majorly, there are 3 OS available in the market. touch-screen are - Windows(from Microsoft), MacOS(from Apple) and Chrome OS(from Google). Each one has its own advantages and disadvantages, but the most-widely used OS in the world is Windows. It is a lot more dynamic than any other OS. It provides quality performance. Users are free to do gigantic customizations and are free to install applications from the web. The latest version of Windows(11) turns out to be the best one so far. In the recent years, Microsoft have done a really good job in their OS development.
    Next comes MacOS, which is developed by Apple, is a bit more oriented towards privacy, security and premiumness, with full of performance. It can be only run of Apple hardware - Macs, Mac Minis and Macbooks. It is no wonder that 80% of content creators prefer MacOS than Windows for video-editing purposed.
    And finally we have the Chrome OS, the newest of all the OS we have discussed. It is developed by Google. This OS looks majorly like windows, but the difference is that it has the ability to run android apps. It is a lightweight OS that hosts most of its tasks in the cloud. This OS does not offer much storage and memory, and most of the time needs an internet connections to work. It is for those users, who have their work on cloud-based applications.


  2. LAPTOP SIZE



    3. It can also be considered as the screen size of the laptop. The bigger the laptop, the bigger the screen. This is an important factor to consider. You should have a clear idea about the purpose for which you are getting a laptop. Is it coding? Gaming? Video-editing? or usual office stuff like Word, Excel or Powerpoint? Well, the most common-sized laptops come in 14"(inches), which are quite comfortable to be used as a lap-top, and are also easy to carry around. They generally weigh around 1-2 Kgs. We also have 13" laptops, that are lighter and very portable. These 13" - 14" sized laptops are ideal for coding and casual office-stuff.
    In many scenarios, users will expect a bigger screen size, i.e, in the case of gaming, content consumption and video-editing. For such customers, brands also offer a 15.6" laptop. They are a bit on the heavier side, generally weighing more than 2 Kgs.
    Laptops bigger than that do exist, which are if the sizes 17" - 18", but then they lose their portability. These laptops become very heavy and are only recommended if you have a surface to kee the device and then work on. This one is definitely not portable.


  3. STORAGE



    4. The next important factor to consider when buying a laptop is its storage. Two thing that you need to check about storage is - what type of storage and how much? There are two types of storage available in the market - HDD(Hard Disk Drive) and SSD(Solid State Drive). The latter is cheap but slow. SSDs are way more faster than the traditional HDDs, but they also come at a good price. A good quality 1TB HDD costs around 4000 INR, whereas the costs go upto 10000 INR when considering SSDs with the same storage capacity. Though the price of an SSD is high, we can't ignore its advatages. The price comes for a reason. SSDs read upto 10x faster and write upto 20x faster than a HDD. They consume less power, they have no mechanical moving parts and thus, do not create noise like HDDs. They boot up the computer in less than 5 seconds, whereas HDDs do it in around 10 - 15 seconds. They are incredibly fast. But, if you want to cut down on the costs, a good HDD might work for you, unless you do not intend to edit videos or run heavy games. One more thing, prefer NVMe SSDs over PCLe SSDs, because NVMe provide more speed in comparison to PCLe SSDs.

    Now let's talk about how much storage your computer should have. The general idea is - The more, the better. A shorter storage like 256 GB means that most of your storage will be occupied by the OS and the applications that you install. Therefore, you will be left with very little for storing documents or multimedia files. But if you often store big-sized files on your laptop, then go for higher storage options like 512 GB or 1 TB. 512 GB is sufficient for a normal user. But in the future, if you require more storage, you might upgrade your laptop's storage or work with an external storage device(like pen drives, external HDDs or SSDs) connected through the USB ports. That's all for the storage part.


  4. PROCESSOR



    5. Next comes the processor, or the CPU(Central Processing Unit) of the laptop. In this sector, there are two leading chip manufacturers. Intel has been in this sector since 1971, way before than AMD, the second most successful company. The most successful Intel processors in use today are - i3, i5, i7 and i9. Intel i9 is the latest and most powerful of all of the above. Intel also has generation of chips, like i5 5th gen, i9 10th gen, i3 11th gen. We should always seek for higher generation of chips. Another thing to keep in mind, i3 11th gen is better than i5 5th gen and likewise.
    As per AMD is concerned, this chip is also known for quality performance, but also causes a bit more heating than Intel chips. AMD chipsets come in series. One should go for the higher series always for better performance. This is the part that will handle all the processes of your computer, so take time to choose a good processor that will suit your needs.


  5. MEMORY (RAM)



    6. The next important part of your laptop is RAM (Random Access Memory). This is that part of your laptop where the applications load into and run. Again, the usual concept comes in, larger the RAM, smoother the functioning of your laptop. Nowadays, general day-to-day tasks can be managed by laptops with 8GB RAM, though 16GB is more preferable. But, such numbers are not sufficient for high-end jobs like gaming, video-editing or graphic designing. For such purposes, you shoulf seeek for a laptop that has at least 32GBs of RAM for a smooth experience.


  6. GRAPHICS PROCESSING UNIT (GPU)



    7. The graphics processing unit, or GPU, has become one of the most important types of computing technology, both for personal and business computing. Designed for parallel processing, the GPU is used in a wide range of applications, including graphics and video rendering. Although they’re best known for their capabilities in gaming, GPUs are becoming more popular for use in creative production and artificial intelligence (AI).
    When looking for graphics cards, there are two main brands you'll choose from; AMD and NVIDIA. Both manufacturers offer high-powered, quality graphics cards.

    1. First, you need to decide on the amount of memory you want in your graphics card.
    2. The higher the storage and model of GPU, the faster will be the processing of Graphics.
    3. Do you want a discrete GPU or graphics card integrated into the CPU?
    4. Take into consideration what power connectors your card uses.
    5. Take note of its thermal design power (TDP).


  7. BATTERY CAPACITY



    8. Another important aspect of a good laptop is a good battery. You are buying a laptop, so you wpuld expect it to have atleast so much of battery capacity that can last for an entire day of work. You would not want to look around for charging sockets when you are out at your work. It should sustain your tasks after you full charge in the morning return home in the evening. So you should prefer those laptops that offer a battery capacity oof at least 8 - 10 hours, not less than that. Because, any person uses his/her laptop on an average for 8 - 10 hours in a day. More than that is definitely good, but not less than that.


  8. YOUR BUDGET



    9. The last and probably the most important thing to consider hen buying a laptop is "Your Budget". You should start with a budget in mind, look for laptops in that budget segment, and finally get one that perfectly suits your needs. If your budget is around 40k, and laptops around 40k do not offer good specs see a 45k laptop to be having the next level of specs that suit your needs, then do not turn back. This is an investment in an asset which, if done wosely, will stay with you for around 10 years. Might go even longer if taken care of well. Therefore, plan everything out wisely and the procedd further.


  9. SOME OTHER FACTORS

    10. Apart from the above main factors, there are some secondary factors than you might want to have a look upon. If you work at night and you aren't aware with touch-typing or the positioning of your keys, you might consider a laptop that comes with a backlit keyboard. You also might want a laptop that can also provide to you the facilities of a touch-screen device. Laptops with touch screens also come that support a bluetooth pen, or just the touch of the fingers. You might also want to take a look at the variety of ports that your laptop offers. A USB type-C, USB type-A, HDMI, Ethernet and headphone jack are some of he ports that people generally look for in a laptop. Thunderbolt ports are always preferred more than normal USB type-C ports because they provide more speed.
